[b]Excerpt from Chronicles of the Shadow Nebula, Book 3, Part 1: Peace Between Heaven and Earth by G. T. Macmillan[/b]

The planet of Samaria was supposed to have been undefended. They were supposed to have been greeted as liberators. It was supposed to be a combination training and humanitarian mission.

Gali could not forget what he saw. The rebel troopers with their pellet guns were no match for Imperial Magi, even magi-in-training. He remembered the glee with which Bruno had hollowed out their bodies, his spells turning their forms to shriveled up husks. He remembered the rebel emerging from the house, no older than a boy, shouldering a gun, frightened expression on his unmasked face, the bullets deflected by Gali's energy shield, his stiff body when the lightning had struck him. But Gali couldn't remember casting the spell.

The Administrator strode forth. Gali confronted him.

*page_break "Why did you lie to us?"

"Why did you lie to us?" asked Gali. "You told us this would be a training mission."

"We had faulty intelligence," the Administrator answered, and Gali could almost believe him. But then the man sighed.

"I needed to show you something. Show all of you." The Administrator continued. "I needed to show you the costs of the Empire's rule. See this? This is what the Emperor has decreed. This is how it always ends."

Gali didn't understand what the man was saying. "What do you mean?" he asked in an accusing voice.

"I mean that..." The Administrator took a long pause, stroking his beard and gazing about the ruins. "I mean that this will never stop. It will only worsen."

*page_break "Traitor."

"Traitor." Bruno suddenly materialized, as if out of nowhere. "I know your game. You're a traitor to the Empire, that's what you are." The blond boy tossed a chunk of ice from one hand to the other, all the while glaring at the Administrator.

The administrator only smiled, returning Bruno's gaze. Bruno's hand flicked. A blue bolt shot forth, only to be met with a red beam from the Administrator's fingers. The magical projectiles collided, exploding in midair, the resulting fireball scorching the grass.

"I could have you executed, Mr. Helios," said the Administrator, not breaking his smile.

"You would kill the scion of the Helios family?" Bruno smirked. "I could end you right now if I expose your plots."

"The nebula is more complicated than you believe, my child. Come. I'll show you." The Administrator beckoned to the two boys.

*page_break Gali followed...

Gail followed the Administrator. Bruno walked in tow, arms folded across his chest. It was not a long walk to the field headquarters, situated in a green tent. Inside, Astra, Capella, and Tycho were already waiting.

"A storm is afoot," said the Administrator in a dark voice. "Forces beyond our mortal comprehension are gathering, and I fear we may be powerless to stop them."

Surprisingly, it was Tycho who stepped in. "So what do you expect us to do? Lay down and die? Let the Demiurge claim us?"

"No," said the Administrator. He paced about his cramped headquarters, stroking his beard. "You do not understand. Perhaps you will never understand."

"So tell us what going on! So at least we can try!" shouted Capella, who had previously been standing on the sidelines.

The Administrator sighed. "Fine, if you so desire."

*page_break Gali watched...

Gail watched intently as the Administrator activated the holographic display on his table. A blue hologram projected upward, showing a map of the Empire's myriad worlds.

The Administrator pointed at the Capitol world of Antioch.

"We are at war with ourselves," he proclaimed. "A shadow movement is brewing against the Emperor, gathering more adherents daily. The Emperor has gone mad in trying to root it out. He has turned our armies against entire worlds. We see not just Samaria, but also Cappadocia, Ephesus, and countless other worlds all burning. This is the world you will enter. This is the lord you will serve."

He takes a deep breath. "And the man leading the shadow movement... is standing before you."

[b]@lunan0va posted at 5:53 PM[/b]

So I've been thinking a lot about death lately.

It wouldn't be the first time I tried. The first time, I just started chewing asprin pills for some reason lol. Barely had a stomachache.

Don't worry, I'm not going to do it.

But there's this bridge I walk across almost every day. It's over a 12-lane highway. And every time I cross it, I look down. I imagine how I would jump down, how I would vault over the railings which are far too low. I imagine what it would be like. Would it be the fall? No, it's too short. Or would it be one of the 18-wheelers? And I remember Leelah Alcorn and I just freeze, and I realize that I don't want to be like her. I don't want to die.

I want to live, but I have no idea how.

It's not so much that I enjoy living. It's more that, well, I'm afraid of what comes next. I don't know if I'm still a Christian but I'm definitely going to hell. And even if not, I'm just going to stop existing, and that might be even scarier. I think we're all afraid of not existing. And, this is ridiculous, but I'm afraid of what would happen to my memory afterwards. There wouldn't be any trace of me left. There wouldn't be any evidence left in the world that I was a woman. That I existed as a trans girl in this world. I don't want to die before I ever existed.

I had an argument with my dad. It wasn't even an argument about me. I didn't try to come out or anything like that (I have some sense of self-preservation after all). It was just a political argument about whether or not trans girls are predators for using women's restrooms. And I just, I just remembered all the messages they sent me, and my dad was basically saying the exact same things that the anons were saying. And I couldn't deal with it anymore, so I got angry, really angry, and yelled at him and said some shitty things and he told me to leave and so I did...

So I'm here in the library. On the library's computer. They're closing in a few hours. I don't know what to do.

I'm scared of a lot of things right now. Like what I'm going to eat. Where I'm going to live. If I have to sleep on the streets in the next two hours.
